Facilities Available

    Manual Therapy

    Manual therapy encompasses various hands-on techniques used by trained therapists to diagnose, treat, and prevent musculoskeletal disorders. It includes joint mobilization, soft tissue mobilization, and manipulation to alleviate pain, improve mobility, and enhance overall physical function. This approach emphasizes personalized care tailored to each individual's specific needs, promoting healing and recovery.

    Hijama Therapy

    Hijama therapy, also known as cupping therapy, is an ancient practice involving the suction of cups on the skin to draw out stagnant blood and toxins. This therapy is believed to stimulate blood flow, reduce inflammation, alleviate pain, and promote overall wellness. It's rooted in traditional medicine and continues to be used for various health conditions.

    Cupping Therapy

    Cupping therapy involves placing cups on the skin to create suction, drawing blood to the surface. This traditional healing method is believed to promote circulation, alleviate pain, and reduce inflammation. It's used worldwide for various conditions, including muscle tension, respiratory issues, and promoting relaxation. Cupping therapy's effectiveness is supported by anecdotal evidence and ongoing research.

    Osteopathy

    Osteopathy is a holistic approach to healthcare that focuses on the body's musculoskeletal system and its connection to overall health. Osteopathic practitioners use manual techniques such as manipulation, stretching, and massage to diagnose and treat various conditions. They aim to restore balance and optimize the body's self-healing mechanisms, promoting overall well-being.

    S.W.D

    Shortwave diathermy (SWD) is a therapeutic modality that uses high-frequency electromagnetic energy to generate heat within deep tissues. This non-invasive treatment is commonly used in physical therapy to relieve pain, reduce muscle spasms, improve blood circulation, and promote tissue healing.

    IFT

    Interferential therapy (IFT) is a type of electrotherapy used in physical therapy for pain management and tissue healing. It involves the use of low-frequency electrical currents to stimulate nerves, reducing pain perception and promoting circulation. IFT is commonly used to treat musculoskeletal injuries and chronic pain conditions.

    UltraSound Therapy

    Ultrasound therapy is a therapeutic technique used in physical therapy to treat musculoskeletal conditions. It involves the use of high-frequency sound waves to penetrate deep into tissues, generating heat and promoting healing. Ultrasound therapy is often used to reduce pain, inflammation, and muscle spasms, as well as to promote tissue repair.

    HydroCollator

    A hydrocollator is a device used in physical therapy clinics to heat and maintain a supply of moist heat packs. These packs, typically filled with silica gel and immersed in water, are heated in the hydrocollator and then applied to the patient's body to help alleviate pain, relax muscles, and improve circulation.
